How We Work

Our trade development process

A structured process helps reduce risk. Every product or supplier opportunity should pass through basic commercial, quality, and compliance checks before execution.

1

Identify Product Opportunity

Shortlist products based on demand, price gap, sourcing availability, quality levels, and market potential.

2

Evaluate Suppliers

Check supplier profile, catalogue, MOQ, payment terms, sample policy, export experience, and certifications.

3

Calculate Landed Cost

Estimate product cost, freight, insurance, duty, GST, CHA charges, port charges, local transport, and margin.

4

Check Compliance

Review HS code, documentation, product certification, packaging requirements, import/export rules, and risk.

5

Begin Trade Engagement

Start serious discussions with suppliers, buyers, freight forwarders, customs brokers, and trade partners.

Product Research

How we evaluate a product before importing

Demand, supplier price, MOQ, landed cost, selling price, margin, certification, and market risk must be checked before selecting a product.

Supplier Verification

Supplier checklist before negotiation

Catalogue, factory details, sample policy, certifications, export history, payment terms, packaging standards, and inspection support are key.

Costing

Why product price is not landed cost

A product can look cheap at supplier price level but become expensive after freight, customs duty, GST, local logistics, and handling charges.

Compliance Awareness

Documentation and regulatory clarity matter

Trade credibility depends on correct documentation, product classification, customs process, payment terms, and transparent communication with partners.

Basic documents we consider

  • Commercial invoice
  • Packing list
  • Bill of lading or airway bill
  • Certificate of origin, if required
  • Inspection certificate, if required
  • Import/export declarations

Key checks before trade

  • HS code classification
  • Customs duty and GST impact
  • Product certification needs
  • Packaging and labelling rules
  • Supplier export readiness
  • Payment and delivery terms
